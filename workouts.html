Workouts

Working out from home can be fun! Below are three different formats for at home exercises that you can do during COVID-19. During this time, it is important that we take care of ourselves physically and mentally. Let's both try to get physically active from our homes! We got this!

Yoga

Mindfulness is important and so is moving your body. With yoga, both mind and body are being cared for. You will be able to feel realxed while still exercising your body! Below are some yoga videos that you can watch and workout to from home. No studio required!

This first video is from Alo Yoga and is a 45 minute power vinyasa flow. To learn more about vinyasa flow click here. You do not necessarily need a yoga mat, but it is reccomended.

The second video is from Yoga Dose and is another 45 minute vinyasa flow yoga class.

The last video is from LuLu Lemon and is a power yoga class. To learn more about power yoga click here.




Indoor Cardio

Is it too cold to go outside? Or are you wanting to stay inside for safety/health reasons? Below are some indoor cardio exercises that you can do at home with little to nothing needed!

This first video an Orange Theory Fitness workout. It is a high intensity interval training combining both dynamic cardio moves and strength with small weights.

The second video is a Pop Sugar Fitness workout that is all standing cardio- no equipment needed.

The last video is also from Pop Sugar Fitness workout and it is a cardio kickboxing class with again no equipment needed.

Strength

Here are a few workout videos for at home strength training. These classes require no equipment, or contain items people typically have around the house. There is also no weights option for every exercise. Get your strength on!

This first video is a strength workout by Joanna Soh Official that uses your own body weight to build strength! Perfect for people wanting to get stronger, but not having enough equipment.

The second video is a strength workout by Jaclyn Wood that is only 25 minutes so perfect if you are working from home and want a quick workout to do.No equipment is needed again!

The last video is also around 25 minutes and is a full-body strength workout by Heather Robertson .
